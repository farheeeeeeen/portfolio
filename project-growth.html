The Problem

EquityList's buyers — founders, CFOs, and HR leads — are solving equity problems in a market that's still new in India. The concepts are unfamiliar, so when a problem comes up, people search to understand it first. That made high-intent search the channel that mattered, and education the way in.

The job: rank for the searches the actual buyer makes — not employees, students, or retail traders — in a niche, still-maturing category against established players, and turn that into demand, not just traffic.

What I Built

A demand engine built on intent, not volume. I targeted keywords the real buyer would search, and went deep on single definitive explainers — betting that one comprehensive, authoritative post could own a result and outperform a hundred shallow ones.

The flagship: a complete, India-focused explainer on dematerialisation under MCA Rule 9b — a niche topic with real intent and almost no competition. It ranked to page one and became the largest single source of organic users on the site.
